What is Eliza?


Eliza is a simulation of a Rogerian psychotherapist, provided a startlingly human-like interaction, written at the MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory by Joseph Weizenbaum between 1964 and 1966. At that time, people started to treat Eliza as a real human psychotherapist and develop emotional linkage to it, which was not Joseph expected. 

What is Google?


Google is a multinational, publicly-traded organization built around the company’s hugely popular search engine. But it has also become the verb means searching information online or the clean search and simple interface. But if we consider the text generated from Google result, they are opinions or thought generated from millions of Internet users online. In this perspective, Google is the window to the internet users' mind.

Google + Eliza

What is Google Eliza Talk?

Google Eliza Talk is a programme that takes “What topic to do you want Google to talk with Eliza” and “How many times do you want Google to talk with Eliza” as input, and create a conversation between Google and Eliza, until the users' IP are blocked by Google. 


From the initial user input, google search the input as keyword and pick one of the sentence from the result pages as input to Eliza. Then Google add the previous result and Eliza’s question as new search keyword. It is a conversation between 2 programme, but it is also organic as the contents are dynamically genereated from the internet users.

Components

Eliza Programme

The Eliza programme is written at MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory by Joseph Weizenbaum. I translated the programme into python logics.

Random Function

The random function is to filter out the sentences from Google search result which contains "…", to prevent the sentence selection return non-complete sentence for the input to Eliza’s question and for the new search.

Text Generation Function

The text generate function use the module of “Request” to get the information from the first page of Google result. And then use module of “Beautiful Soup” to get the text which in the class “st” in the Google search result page. 


Sentiment Function

The sentiment function takes all the text from the first page from Google result as input and calculate the polarity and subjectivity of those text. Thus display the emotion of Google. 



Console Function

The console function collect the input from the users, i.e. "What do you want Google to discuss with Eliza" and "How many times do you want Google to talk with Eliza?" or to quit the programme. It prints out what Eliza and Google said for every 2 seconds. 


Future Functions




With the TextBlob module, it is easy to detect the words in terms of phrases, verbs, objects, adjectives, etc. The programme can display the pitures of the locations, adjectives, and nouns, etc, when Google is responding to Eliza, makes the conversation be more human-like. 






With the TextBlob module, it can achieve sentiment score analysis, to give the emotion to the results from Google. It can be liked to my last project – Emotional Identifier, to express the emotion from Google by lights.
